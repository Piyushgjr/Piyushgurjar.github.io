About Me

I am a microbiologist with research interests in environmental biotechnology, environmental microbiology, microalgal biotechnology, waste valorisation and sustainable bioprocess engineering. My work focuses on developing environmentally sustainable technologies that convert biological resources and waste streams into valuable products while supporting the principles of the circular bioeconomy.

My academic and research experience combines microbiology, analytical chemistry and computational biology. During my Integrated BS–MS programme, I investigated the biodegradation of the plasticiser Di-(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP) using bacterial isolates and explored enzyme engineering through molecular docking and protein modelling. This interdisciplinary project strengthened my understanding of microbial metabolism, environmental remediation and bioinformatics.

Currently, as a Project Associate-I at CSIR–Central Institute of Medicinal and Aromatic Plants (CSIR-CIMAP), I am involved in developing sustainable technologies for the valorisation of aromatic plant processing waste, microalgal cultivation and production of high-value bioproducts. My long-term goal is to contribute to innovative research addressing global environmental challenges through biotechnology and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Research Philosophy & Career Vision

Research Philosophy

I believe that biotechnology should contribute directly to solving real-world environmental challenges. My research philosophy emphasizes integrating microbiology, environmental biotechnology, computational biology and sustainable bioprocess engineering to develop innovative, economically viable and environmentally responsible technologies. I am particularly interested in transforming biological systems into sustainable solutions for pollution remediation, waste valorisation and renewable resource generation while supporting the principles of the circular bioeconomy.
