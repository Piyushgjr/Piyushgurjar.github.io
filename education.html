Master's Thesis

Bacterial Degradation of Di-(2-ethylhexyl) Phthalate (DEHP) and In Silico Analysis of Interaction with Carboxylesterase Est2


Plastic pollution has become one of the major environmental challenges worldwide. Di-(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP) is one of the most widely used plasticizers and is frequently detected in soil, air and aquatic environments. The objective of this research was to investigate microbial degradation of DEHP using efficient bacterial strains and understand the molecular mechanism responsible for degradation through computational biology approaches.


🎯 Research Objectives

  • Isolate efficient DEHP-degrading bacteria.
  • Evaluate degradation efficiency.
  • Identify degradation enzymes.
  • Study enzyme–substrate interactions.
  • Engineer improved enzyme variants.

🧪 Methodology

  • Bacterial isolation and cultivation.
  • Biodegradation experiments.
  • Growth kinetics analysis.
  • Protein modelling.
  • Molecular docking using AutoDock.

🔬 Key Findings

  • Efficient bacterial strains identified.
  • Consortium improved degradation.
  • Carboxylesterase Est2 characterized.
  • Engineered variants showed improved binding.
  • Integrated microbiology with computational biology.
